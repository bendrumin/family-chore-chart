## Using GIFs in Email Newsletters

**How animated GIFs work in email**

Animated GIFs work natively in most modern email clients and newsletter platforms â€” Mailchimp, Beehiiv, and Substack all support them without any special configuration. You insert the GIF like a regular image, and it plays automatically for most subscribers. One important caveat: **Outlook on Windows (2013â€“2019) does not animate GIFs** â€” it shows only the first frame as a static image. **Target file size: under 1MB**

File size is the thing that trips most people up with GIFs in email. Aim to keep each GIF **under 1MB** â€” this ensures fast loading even on mobile. Up to **2MB is generally acceptable**, but expect slower loads. For product demo GIFs at typical email widths (600px), you can usually hit under 1MB by limiting the animation to 3â€“6 seconds and keeping the frame rate around 8â€“15fps. Tools like Ezgif, Gifski, or Squoosh can help compress without killing quality.

**GIF vs. static image with a play button**

For highly polished or longer demos, consider using a **static thumbnail with a play button overlay**, linked to the hosted MP4 (or YouTube). This works in every email client, loads instantly, and drives click-through traffic. The tradeoff is an extra click. For short 2â€“4 second loops (like the avatar Randomize button), an inline GIF is usually the better choice â€” it delivers the "wow" immediately. For longer walkthroughs (like the full routine completion), a linked thumbnail wins.
